Small Mim

In the Holy Quran, small mim is sometime found printed on top of letter Nun, bearing Jazm when its following letter is 'ba'. In this case 'Mim' is pronounced instead of Nun.

دَرْسٌ نَمْبَر ٣٧

Wuqoof - Stops

○ = Ayya sign  |  ◌ = Compulsory stop sign  |  ◌ = Optional stop sign  |  ◌ = General stop sign. When any of these stop signs is met with during the course of recitation of Quran, there are certain rules to follow in order to stop there: (1) If the word ends in Jazm there is no modification of sound at the stop. (2) If the word ends with a sign which is not Jazm, it is changed to Jazm which then connects the proceeding letter. (3) If it is regarded to be Ending, changes to (ha with jazm). (4) Blank Aliph with a double Fatha before it changes to (na with jazm). (5) If the word ends in blank Aliph, but there is no double Fatha, Fatha and Aliph remains blank. (6) If the word ends in blank 'ya' and the sign before it, there is no change in sound at the stop. (7) If the preceding it is double Fatha, then ya changes to Aliph and double Fatha to simple Fatha. (8) If the word ends in blank 'ya' and there is vertical Fatha before it, there is no change of sound at the stop. Examples are given below together with equivalent Arabic in each case.
