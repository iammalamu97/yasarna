دَرْسٌ نَمْبَر ٣٩

Small 'seen or nun'

B. Small print nun ن is sometimes written on the top of a 'Nun'. These are read as two separate 'Nun'.

Examples: نَحِي المُومِنِين is pronounced نُنجِي المُومِنِين (Nunjilmo' minin).

دَرْسٌ نَمْبَر ٤٠

Aliph Zaida

In the Holy Quran, there are several places where there is a blank Aliph and there is a Fatha before it and no Jazm or shadda after it. This, according to lesson 19 and 23 requires that Aliph be pronounced. But in these places it is not pronounced. Therefore such Aliph is called 'Aliph zaida'.

Example: اَفَنَا مَات is only read as اَفَنَمَات. All such places where Aliph is superfluous are marked.

رُكُوع آيَت نَامِ سُورَه پَارَه شُمَار رُكُوع آيَت نَامِ سُورَه پَارَه شُمَار

مَلَّت ٢ — Everywhere in the Holy Quran is to be read مَلَّت
اَن ١ — Everywhere in the Holy Quran is to be read اَن
